What is Reiki

Life force, in balance

Ki (気) is the Japanese word for life force energy — the vital current that flows through every living being. The practice of reiki, rooted in the Usui Shiki Ryoho tradition, works by channelling this energy through the practitioner's hands to support the body's natural ability to heal.

When ki flows freely, the body and mind find equilibrium. Stress, injury, emotional strain, and accumulated tension can all disrupt this flow — creating blockages that manifest as anxiety, disrupted sleep, or persistent physical pain.

A reiki session doesn't diagnose or treat. It creates the conditions for your nervous system to settle, your body to release what it's holding, and your natural rhythms to reassert themselves. Most clients notice a deep sense of calm during the session itself; lasting effects develop over a course of treatments.

Anxiety
Calms an overactive nervous system; reduces the grip of worry and hypervigilance.
Sleep
Supports deeper, more restorative rest by easing the transition into parasympathetic states.
Muscular Pain
Encourages tension release in areas of chronic or acute physical holding.
